    <p>
      The research peptide space is written almost entirely for one audience: male athletes. The language is gym-coded, the framing assumes a performance goal, and the research areas that matter to a significant share of actual researchers (skin, aging, weight, hormonal health, hair, longevity) are either missing or buried.
    </p>
    <p>
      This page collects the peptides with substantial research interest in areas relevant to women, and links to research notes written to reflect who actually searches for this information. The evidence base varies considerably across these compounds: from FDA-approved pharmaceuticals to peptides studied primarily in cell cultures. Each page is honest about where the research stands.
    </p>

  <section class="research-area">
    <h2>Skin, Collagen, and Anti-Aging Research</h2>
    <p class="area-desc">These peptides appear most frequently in research on skin remodeling, collagen synthesis, and cellular aging. The evidence base is primarily in vitro and animal models, with cosmetic applications more studied than systemic ones.</p>
    <div class="peptide-cards">

      <div class="peptide-card">
        <div class="card-content">
          <div class="card-name">
            GHK-Cu
            <span class="badge badge-live">Research Notes Live</span>
          </div>
          <p class="card-desc">Copper peptide with one of the largest in vitro evidence bases in skin research. Studies have examined its role in collagen synthesis, fibroblast activation, and gene expression patterns linked to aging. Serum levels decline significantly with age, from roughly 200 ng/ml at 20 to 80 ng/ml by 60.</p>
        </div>

            <span class="badge badge-soon">Coming Soon</span>

          <p class="card-desc">A synthetic tetrapeptide studied in the context of telomere biology and cellular longevity. Research has examined its potential role in influencing telomerase activity and age-related tissue changes, primarily in animal models and some older human studies conducted in Russia.</p>

  <section class="research-area">
    <h2>Weight and Metabolic Health Research</h2>
    <p class="area-desc">GLP-1 receptor agonists are the most clinically advanced peptides in this category. Both have FDA-approved pharmaceutical forms available by prescription; the research notes on these pages cover the compound class as studied in published literature.</p>
    <div class="peptide-cards">

      <div class="peptide-card">
        <div class="card-content">
          <div class="card-name">
            Semaglutide
            <span class="badge badge-live">Research Notes Live</span>
            <span class="badge badge-fda">FDA-Approved</span>
          </div>
          <p class="card-desc">GLP-1 receptor agonist studied for weight management, metabolic health, and cardiovascular outcomes. The landmark STEP 1 trial demonstrated significant weight reduction; the SELECT trial showed cardiovascular risk reduction in people with obesity. Marketed as Ozempic (T2D) and Wegovy (obesity).</p>

    <h2>Hormonal Health and Libido Research</h2>
    <p class="area-desc">PT-141 is the most clinically advanced peptide in this category with a women-specific FDA approval. It is the only research peptide approved specifically for a condition defined in women.</p>
    <div class="peptide-cards">

      <div class="peptide-card">
        <div class="card-content">

            <span class="badge badge-soon">Coming Soon</span>

          <p class="card-desc">Melanocortin receptor agonist approved by the FDA in 2019 as Vyleesi specifically for hypoactive sexual desire disorder (HSDD) in premenopausal women. This is the only research peptide with a women-specific FDA indication. Existing content on PT-141 almost entirely ignores this context.</p>
